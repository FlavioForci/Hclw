Lost Sword Beginner Guide

Beginner Guide

Guide Overview

This guide provides gameplay tips for Lost Sword based on real playthroughs.

Written from hands-on experience; strategies may vary depending on luck or circumstances.

Treat this as a flexible reference, not an absolute rulebook.

How to Start?

Is Rerolling Essential?

Not mandatory, but progression may stall later.

4★ characters (with level caps) can only carry you so far.

Recommendation: Reroll if possible.

Playstyle

Lost Sword is an idle RPG. If you get stuck in one mode, switch to another to strengthen your team.

Progression relies on cycling through three key content types:

Level Scaling: Enemies gain a "Level Compensation" buff if your team’s average level is lower.

Team Building

Ideal Start: Reroll until your Team is missing only 1 core character (e.g., Galahad).

Aim for 3-4 strong units; a perfect 5-unit team is time-consuming.

Team building tips

  • The five-star character basically has four skills.

  • Sixty skill pages are required to enhance each skill.

  • However, in the beginning, it is not easy to secure a skill page.

  • It is important to proceed with skill enhancements carefully.

Christina Passive: Allies Attack Damage 36% (Lv.3)

Merlin Passive: 10% critical hit increase (Lv.3)

Ria Passive: Critical Damage increase 40% (Lv.3)

Isolde Active: Ally Damage Reduction 12% (Lv.3)

  • Level up skills that increase "party-wide stats"

  • Expecting an overall combat strength boost can be expected to be much higher efficiency in the long run.
